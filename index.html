About Amir Shapiro

Amir Shapiro is a Full Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev (BGU), Israel. He is the founder and director of the BGU Robotics Laboratory. His research interests include robot grasp planning and control, object manipulation, humanoid robot locomotion, rehabilitation robotics, agriculture robotics, and climbing robots.

Prof. Shapiro has made significant contributions to the field of robotics, particularly in the areas of grasp stability analysis, motion planning for walking and climbing robots, and the development of unique robotic systems for various applications. He has published extensively in top robotics journals and conferences and holds several patents for his inventions.

In addition to his research activities, Prof. Shapiro is a dedicated educator, teaching undergraduate and graduate courses in robotics and mentoring numerous student projects. He is also actively involved in promoting robotics education and outreach.
